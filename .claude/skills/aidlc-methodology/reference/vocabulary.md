# AI-DLC vocabulary

AI-DLC renames several Agile concepts to reflect an AI-accelerated cadence and a
collaboration-first stance. Use these terms consistently in product-facing
methodology content.

## Glossary

**Bolt**
: The AI-DLC unit of cadence, replacing the **sprint**. Measured in **hours to
  days**, not weeks — short and intense. Work is scoped to fit a bolt.

**Unit of Work**
: Replaces the **epic**. A chunk of value decomposed during Inception, sized to be
  developed in parallel within a bolt.

**Mob Elaboration**
: The **Inception** ceremony, replacing traditional requirements meetings. The team
  collaboratively validates AI's proposed requirements and answers its clarifying
  questions in real time.

**Mob Construction**
: The **Construction** ceremony, replacing design/build-planning meetings. The team
  makes real-time technical decisions and validates AI's proposed architecture,
  models, code, and tests.

**Arbiter (the human)**
: Not a renamed Agile term but the central role. Humans are the arbiter of critical
  decisions: AI plans, asks, and implements, but humans hold the business context,
  the decision-making **authority**, and the **accountability**. AI proceeds to
  implementation only after human validation.

## Agile mapping

| Traditional Agile | AI-DLC term | Key shift |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sprint | **Bolt** | Weeks → hours/days |
| Epic | **Unit of Work** | Sized for parallel AI-driven development |
| Requirements meeting | **Mob Elaboration** | AI proposes; team validates collectively |
| Design / planning meeting | **Mob Construction** | Real-time technical decisions with AI |
| Product owner / decision-maker | **Arbiter** | Human retains authority + accountability |

## Community gloss

The mob ceremonies are sometimes summarized as **"extreme decision-making via mob
work"** — collective intelligence applied fast, with AI accelerating and humans
deciding.
